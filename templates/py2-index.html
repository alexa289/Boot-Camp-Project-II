Data Discovery

Findings in the Dataset


1.There are 42 countries in our dataset. However, only 10 countries account for 96% of the reviews. The US itself accounts for over 40% of reviews and England has the highest-rated wines on average, but with only 60 wines (0.04% of total) so its not a significant representation of the more than 11k reviews in this dataset

2.France and US have most expensive wines at 3300 and 2013 a bottle, respectively, but this represents only1% of the dataset 99% of it has prices below $200.

3.20% of wine reviews come from anonymous tasters and also 20% of wine reviews come from a the same taster, @vossroger. Also the top tasters usually focuses on wines from one country, not tasting other countries. This may have biassed the results of this dataset.

4.Price has over than 8k missing values with a heavy number of outliers.

5.The majority of wines are rated between 87 and 91 and cost between $25-$42.

6.There is a high correlation between price and points. However, we can't simply determine the price with a linear expression of its ratings.
